# Introduction

## Background

Mobile money transformed financial inclusion in Uganda, enabling millions of people to send and receive money, pay bills, and conduct business digitally. According to the Uganda Communications Commission (UCC), over 30 million mobile money accounts were active nationwide, led by MTN Mobile Money and Airtel Money.

This growth came with a rise in fraud — fake SMS alerts, impersonation calls, and deceptive PIN requests — which disproportionately affected students and small business owners, especially in urban communities like Kampala. National campaigns such as **Tonfera** (UCC) and **Beera Steady** (launched 2023) promoted financial literacy through mass media, but remained largely passive, offering little opportunity for practical, hands-on response training. CyberScam Lab was designed to close this gap, aligning with Uganda's Digital Transformation Roadmap (FY2023/24–2027/28), which emphasizes digital safety and citizen empowerment.

As Albert Bandura, a leading psychologist in behavioral learning, observed, learning is far more effective when people can practice and observe modeled scenarios rather than relying solely on the consequences of their own real-world mistakes. This insight underpins CyberScam Lab's design: a mobile app that uses voice-guided scenarios, decision-making modules, and feedback tools to train users in fraud awareness.

## Statement of the Problem

Mobile money fraud in Kampala became increasingly sophisticated, targeting vulnerable users who lacked the practical knowledge to detect and respond to fake SMS alerts or impersonation calls. Despite national campaigns, most interventions remained theoretical or media-based — there was no locally relevant mobile application that simulated real-world scam experiences. Without such a tool, mobile money users faced continued financial loss, emotional distress, and eroding trust in digital financial services.

**In short:** mobile money users in Kampala remained vulnerable to fraud due to limited access to practical, simulation-based digital safety education.

## General Objective

To design and develop CyberScam Lab, a simulation-based mobile learning platform that equips mobile money users with practical fraud awareness skills through interactive scam scenarios.

## Specific Objectives

1. Study common mobile money fraud experiences and digital safety gaps among users in Kampala.
2. Analyse the findings and determine user and system requirements for a simulation-based mobile money fraud awareness platform.
3. Design CyberScam Lab based on the identified requirements, including UI mockups, scenario flows, and voice scripts.
4. Develop CyberScam Lab as a mobile application using Flutter and Dart, integrating interactive modules and voice simulations.
5. Test and validate CyberScam Lab through user feedback, scenario accuracy, and usability evaluation.

## Scope

- **Technical scope:** Six modules — Scam Simulation, Decision Tracking, Video Viewing, Progress Monitoring, Settings, and Backup — built with Flutter and Dart, tested on Android devices.
- **Geographic scope:** Kampala and neighbouring areas where mobile money use and fraud are common.
- **Time scope:** October 2025 – June 2026.
- **Testing sample:** Students, market vendors, and boda-boda riders in Kampala.

## Significance of the Study

- **Mobile money users:** A safe environment to practice responding to scams without risking real money.
- **Teachers and trainers:** A practical tool for teaching fraud awareness in schools and community programmes.
- **Developers and researchers:** A demonstration of how mobile apps can support practical digital safety education.

**Market survey and viability:** The application targets Kampala's mobile money users, particularly students and small business owners most affected by fraud. To ensure viability without charging users directly, the project is designed to work through strategic partnerships with telecom companies, NGOs, and government digital-safety initiatives — aligning with national goals for inclusive technology adoption.

## Justification / Rationale

CyberScam Lab provides a safe, practical way for users to learn how scams work — a gap mass-media campaigns can't fill. Without it, users would continue falling victim to increasingly sophisticated fraud, further eroding trust in Uganda's digital financial ecosystem. The project supports national efforts to improve digital safety and financial protection.
